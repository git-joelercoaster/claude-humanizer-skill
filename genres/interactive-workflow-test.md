# Extended Pattern Library: 20+ AI Tells

---

## CATEGORY 1: INFLATED SIGNIFICANCE

**Tell:** Turning mundane facts into grand narratives.

### 1.1 Significance Puffing

**Keywords:** stands as, serves as, testament to, reminder of, crucial/pivotal/vital/significant role, underscores the importance of, highlights its impact, marked a shift, shaped the evolution

**Problem:** LLMs add meaning that isn't there. They dress up simple facts in robes.

**Before:**
> The coffee shop opened in 2015, marking a pivotal moment in the revitalization of downtown commerce. This establishment stands as a testament to the city's commitment to urban renewal.

**After:**
> The coffee shop opened in 2015. It was one of several new businesses downtown that year.

---

### 1.2 Undue Emphasis on Legacy & Legacy-Building

**Keywords:** enduring legacy, lasting impact, transformed the landscape, revolutionized, left an indelible mark, shaping future, for generations to come

**Problem:** AI assumes everything is historically significant. Most things aren't.

**Before:**
> Her volunteer work has left an indelible mark on the community and will shape its future for generations to come.

**After:**
> She volunteered for three years and helped organize the food bank.

---

### 1.3 "Reflects Broader Trends"

**Keywords:** reflects broader trends, exemplifies a shift in, part of a larger movement, contributes to the growing

**Problem:** AI connects everything to macro narratives. Humans just say what happened.

**Before:**
> The rise of remote work exemplifies a broader shift in how companies structure their operations, contributing to the growing decentralization of the traditional office.

**After:**
> More companies let people work from home now.

---

## CATEGORY 2: FAKE URGENCY & ARTIFICIAL STAKES

**Tell:** Manufactured drama. Manufactured time pressure.

### 2.1 Urgency Markers

**Keywords:** demands attention, cannot be overstated, absolutely critical, must be addressed immediately, is at risk, cannot afford to ignore, the stakes are high

**Problem:** AI adds urgency to routine claims to make them sound important.

**Before:**
> The quarterly budget review cannot be overstated in its importance and must be completed by Friday.

**After:**
> The quarterly budget review is due Friday.

---

### 2.2 Artificial Consequences Inflation

**Keywords:** could have catastrophic implications, risks being, threatens to, jeopardizes the, puts at risk

**Problem:** AI inflates stakes. A typo becomes a crisis. A disagreement becomes a rift.

**Before:**
> Failing to implement proper data backups could have catastrophic implications for the company's future operations.

**After:**
> Without proper data backups, you could lose customer data.

---

## CATEGORY 3: CARGO-CULT BUZZWORDS

**Tell:** Jargon that sounds good but means nothing.

### 3.1 Tech/Startup Buzzwords

**Keywords:** synergy, leverage, seamlessly integrate, unlock potential, scale, disrupt/disruption, synergistic, optimize, paradigm shift, thought leadership, circle back, circle back around, do a deep dive, vertically integrated, best-of-breed

**Problem:** AI defaults to startup vocabulary. Even when it doesn't fit.

**Before:**
> We need to leverage our core competencies to unlock synergistic value and scale our impact.

**After:**
> We need to use what we're good at to grow.

---

### 3.2 Corporate Hedging Buzzwords

**Keywords:** stakeholder alignment, touch base, going forward, at the end of the day, at this point in time, in the final analysis, to be perfectly honest

**Problem:** Buzzwords that sound thoughtful but add no information.

**Before:**
> Going forward, we need to achieve greater stakeholder alignment to ensure the sustainability of our initiatives.

**After:**
> The team needs to agree on next steps.

---

## CATEGORY 4: HEDGING QUALIFIERS (THE OVERQUALIFIER)

**Tell:** Excessive hedging. LLM anxiety made visible.

### 4.1 Introductory Hedges

**Keywords:** it's worth noting that, it's important to mention that, one could argue that, it should be noted that, it could be said that, arguably

**Problem:** These add no information and make the writer sound uncertain.

**Before:**
> It's worth noting that the results suggest that, arguably, the approach may have merit.

**After:**
> The results suggest the approach worked.

---

### 4.2 Qualifier Stacking

**Keywords:** might potentially, could arguably, may possibly, seems to suggest that, appears to indicate

**Problem:** Multiple hedges = zero commitment.

**Before:**
> This could potentially suggest that the approach might possibly lead to improvements.

**After:**
> This approach could improve things.

---

## CATEGORY 5: THESAURUS ABUSE

**Tell:** Reaching for fancy words when simple ones exist.

### 5.1 Synonym Escalation

**Problem:** Replacing common words with unnecessarily elaborate ones.

**Before:**
> We observed a magnificent vista of the ocean juxtaposed against the verdant hills.

**After:**
> We saw the ocean next to green hills.

**Before:**
> The project encountered multitudinous challenges requiring expeditious resolution.

**After:**
> The project had lots of problems that needed quick fixes.

---

### 5.2 Fancified Everyday Words

**Keywords:** commenced (started), endeavor (try), utilize (use), facilitate (help), implement (do), leverage (use), propitious (good), felicitous (lucky), burgeoning (growing), nascent (new)

**Problem:** Swapping simple words for fancy ones looks like insecurity.

**Before:**
> We will endeavor to facilitate a propitious outcome by leveraging our core competencies.

**After:**
> We'll try to help and do well using what we're good at.

---

## CATEGORY 6: FAKE CANDOR

**Tell:** False intimacy. Manufactured honesty before a routine claim.

### 6.1 Theatrical Pause Candor

**Keywords:** Honestly? Let me tell you. Here's the thing. To be honest. Real talk. The truth is. Look.

**Problem:** A one-word opener + pause + reveal = fake intimacy. Real honesty just says the thing.

**Before:**
> Honestly? The project is behind schedule.

**After:**
> The project is behind schedule.

**Before:**
> Here's the thing — AI is actually pretty useful.

**After:**
> AI is useful.

---

### 6.2 Implied Confidence with Low Evidence

**Keywords:** clearly, obviously, evidently, it goes without saying, needless to say

**Problem:** Asserting obviousness when the claim isn't obvious at all.

**Before:**
> Obviously, remote work is the future.

**After:**
> Remote work is becoming more common.

---

## CATEGORY 7: PERFORMATIVE CASUALNESS

**Tell:** Forced informality. LLM trying on a "casual" voice and failing.

### 7.1 Unnatural Colloquialisms

**Keywords:** lol, literally, just, so, basically, honestly, like, vibe, lowkey, highkey, no cap

**Problem:** Used awkwardly, out of context, forced.

**Before:**
> Honestly, this project is literally just so important, like, no cap.

**After:**
> This project matters.

---

### 7.2 Fake Relatability

**Keywords:** We all know, Everyone agrees, As we've all experienced, Obviously

**Problem:** Assumes agreement that doesn't exist.

**Before:**
> As we all know, productivity has become an obsession in modern culture.

**After:**
> Productivity culture has become common.

---

## CATEGORY 8: STACCATO DRAMA

**Tell:** Three short punchy sentences in a row to manufacture urgency.

### 8.1 Rhythm Manipulation

**Problem:** Multiple short fragments in sequence = fake drama.

**Before:**
> The deadline is Friday. No extensions. We need results.

**After:**
> The deadline is Friday. We can't extend it.

---

### 8.2 Orphan Fragments for Emphasis

**Keywords:** [Short sentence alone.] [Another short sentence.] [A third.]

**Problem:** Overuse flattens actual emphasis.

**Before:**
> The market is shifting. Competition is rising. We must act now.

**After:**
> The market is shifting and competition is rising. We need to move fast.

---

## CATEGORY 9: PASSIVE VOICE PILEUPS

**Tell:** Hiding the actor. Making claims sound more authoritative by removing agency.

### 9.1 Actorless Construction

**Keywords:** was conducted, was found, is known to, can be seen, has been shown, is believed to, is thought to

**Problem:** Removes accountability. Who conducted it? Who knows? AI hides it.

**Before:**
> It is believed that the approach may yield positive results.

**After:**
> We think the approach will work.

**Before:**
> The experiment was conducted to determine efficacy.

**After:**
> We ran the experiment to test if it worked.

---

### 9.2 Indefinite Agents

**Keywords:** one might argue, some suggest, critics point out, experts agree

**Problem:** Fake attribution. Who is one? Which experts?

**Before:**
> One might suggest that remote work has its challenges.

**After:**
> Remote work has challenges.

---

## CATEGORY 10: RULE-OF-THREE RHYTHMS

**Tell:** Three-part lists beating the reader over the head.

### 10.1 Triplet Beatdowns

**Keywords:** [X], [Y], and [Z]; beauty, grace, and elegance; innovation, excellence, and impact

**Problem:** Three-part lists feel formulaic when they're the main rhythm.

**Before:**
> This project demonstrates innovation, excellence, and impact.

**After:**
> This project is innovative and excellent.

**Before:**
> The company values integrity, innovation, and impact.

**After:**
> The company values integrity and innovation.

---

## CATEGORY 11: EM-DASH OVERUSE

**Tell:** Em dashes — used constantly — to — add — drama.

### 11.1 Dash Staccato

**Keywords:** — (appearing 3+ times in a paragraph)

**Problem:** Interruption pattern becomes visible. Sounds like AI caught mid-thought.

**Before:**
> The project — while challenging — has demonstrated — through multiple iterations — that the approach works.

**After:**
> The project, though challenging, has shown the approach works.

---

## CATEGORY 12: VAGUE ATTRIBUTION

**Tell:** Claims about what "many people" think without evidence.

### 12.1 Unspecified Sources

**Keywords:** many people, some say, experts agree, research shows, it's commonly believed, conventional wisdom holds

**Problem:** Makes claims sound sourced when they're not.

**Before:**
> Many experts agree that AI will transform society.

**After:**
> AI will likely change how we work.

---

### 12.2 False Consensus

**Keywords:** as everyone knows, it's a well-known fact, nobody disputes, universal agreement

**Problem:** Assuming agreement for rhetorical effect.

**Before:**
> As everyone knows, climate change is the most pressing issue of our time.

**After:**
> Climate change is a major issue.

---

## CATEGORY 13: FORCED RESOLUTION OF TENSION

**Tell:** Opening with ambiguity or conflict, then too-neatly resolving it.

### 13.1 False Conflict Setup

**Problem:** "You'd think X, but actually Y!" manufactured drama.

**Before:**
> You might think AI is just hype. But in reality, it's transforming entire industries.

**After:**
> AI is transforming industries.

---

### 13.2 Tension That Never Existed

**Problem:** Setting up a problem that isn't real, then "solving" it.

**Before:**
> Many worry that remote work reduces productivity. However, studies show that's not the case.

**After:**
> Remote work doesn't reduce productivity.

---

## CATEGORY 14: OVERSTATED PERSONAL STAKES

**Tell:** Treating personal anecdotes like life-changing moments.

### 14.1 Mundane Elevated to Transformative

**Keywords:** changed my life, never looked back, fundamental shift, learned a crucial lesson, it was a turning point

**Problem:** AI treats regular events as profound. Humans just say what happened.

**Before:**
> I decided to wake up early every day, and it fundamentally transformed my entire existence. This practice changed my life in ways I never imagined.

**After:**
> Waking up early helped me get more done.

---

## CATEGORY 15: MANUFACTURED NOSTALGIA

**Tell:** Fake wistfulness. Reaching for emotional resonance that isn't there.

### 15.1 Artificial "Remember When"

**Keywords:** simpler times, back when, remember when, in those days, the good old days

**Problem:** Nostalgia for things that weren't actually good, or didn't happen.

**Before:**
> In those simpler times, companies valued long-term relationships and genuine human connection.

**After:**
> Some older companies prioritized long-term relationships.

---

## CATEGORY 16: GENERIC UPBEAT CONCLUSIONS

**Tell:** Ending every piece with "the best is yet to come."

### 16.1 False Hope Closings

**Keywords:** only time will tell, the possibilities are endless, bright future ahead, exciting times ahead, stay tuned

**Problem:** Every piece ends the same way: vaguely positive, commitment-free.

**Before:**
> As we look to the future, the possibilities are truly endless, and I'm excited to see what comes next.

**After:**
> [End with what actually happened, not what might happen]

---

## CATEGORY 17: EMOJI AS PUNCTUATION

**Tell:** Using emoji to add emotional weight that words didn't earn.

### 17.1 Unearned Sentimentality

**Keywords:** ✨ ❤️ 🚀 💡 🎉

**Problem:** Emoji in formal writing signals "I don't trust my words."

**Before:**
> I'm so excited about this project! 🚀 ✨

**After:**
> I'm excited about this project.

---

## CATEGORY 18: CURLY QUOTES + OTHER TELLS STACKED

**Tell:** Multiple tells in close proximity = AI.

### 18.1 The Cluster Effect

**Problem:** One curly quote alone means nothing. But curly quote + em dash + rule-of-three + "vibrant tapestry" = confession.

**Example cluster (all tells together):**
> "The community stands as a vibrant testament to human resilience, with beauty, grace, and authenticity woven throughout — creating, in essence, a truly transformative space for all." ✨

---

## CATEGORY 19: UNNECESSARY EMPHASIS ON COVERAGE

**Tell:** Proving notability by citing media mentions.

### 19.1 "As Featured In" Syndrome

**Keywords:** independent coverage, local media outlets, has been featured in, has gained attention from, written by a leading expert, garnered significant media attention

**Problem:** LLM tries to establish credibility by listing sources. Doesn't work if sources are irrelevant.

**Before:**
> The initiative has garnered significant media attention from regional outlets and was featured in several local publications.

**After:**
> [Skip this entirely or say what the actual impact was]

## CATEGORY 20: AI-SPECIFIC VOCABULARY MARKERS

**Tell:** Words and phrases AI uses more than humans.

### 20.1 Signature AI Verbs & Nouns

**Keywords:** facilitate, leverage, curate, empower, unlock, synergy, paradigm, ecosystem, stakeholder, actionable insights, key learnings, impact, game-changing, cutting-edge, innovative, transformative

**Problem:** These cluster in AI writing. Humans use them occasionally; AI defaults to them.

**Before:**
> We need to empower stakeholders to unlock key learnings and drive actionable insights across the ecosystem.

**After:**
> We need to share what we learned with the team.

---

### 20.2 Signature AI Sentence Starters

**Keywords:** It's important to note that, One might argue that, One could suggest that, It could be said that, Additionally, Furthermore, Notably

**Problem:** Human writing varies openings. AI defaults to these.

**Before (AI):**
> Additionally, it's important to note that the findings suggest transformative potential. Furthermore, one might argue that the implications are significant.

**After (Human):**
> The findings look promising. They could matter.

---

## Detection Rules

**Cluster matters, not isolation:**
- One em dash? Human.
- Em dash + rule-of-three + "vibrant" + Conclusion section? AI.

**Look for:**
- 3+ tells in one paragraph = suspicious
- 5+ across the piece = very likely AI
- Pattern clustering across whole doc = almost certain

**Trust your gut:**
- If it reads like a template, it probably is
- If it sounds like it's convincing you of something you already know, AI probably wrote it
- If every sentence feels like it's auditioning for a TED talk, AI.

---

## False Positives to Avoid

**Don't flag:**
- A single fancy word (not all "leverage" is AI)
- Passive voice in technical writing (appropriate)
- One hedging phrase (humans do this)
- Curly quotes alone (OS default)
- A single short sentence (emphasis is human)
- Unsourced claims (most web is unsourced)

**Look for context.** Isolated tells are noise. Clusters are signals.
