# Genre Profile: Social & LinkedIn Posts

LinkedIn posts. Twitter/X threads. Personal brand. Professional social updates. Reflections. Wins. Lessons learned.

## The Goal

**Personality + substance. Authentic wins. Avoid the performance.**

LinkedIn and social media bring out the worst in AI writing because the culture is performative. AI amplifies the performance.

Real social writing: specific, honest, slightly vulnerable, genuine.

## What Changes

### DO Strip

- Emoji as punctuation (🚀 ✨ 💡 🔥 🌟)
- Motivational boilerplate ("believe in yourself")
- Rule-of-three virtues/lessons
- Fake humility ("I can't believe I...")
- Manufactured vulnerability
- Hashtag abuse
- Generic lessons that don't connect to the story
- "Look how wise I am" framing
- Upbeat generic conclusions

### DO NOT Strip

- Personality (this is the whole point)
- Genuine emotion
- Honest wins (brag a little)
- Specific stories
- Real challenges
- Personality quirks
- Humor
- Emoji used *sparingly* and *meaningfully*

## Signs of AI on Social

**Problem areas:**
- "Just hit a major milestone! 🚀 I'm thrilled to share that our team has achieved something truly transformative."
- "Three reasons I succeeded: (1) Belief, (2) Persistence, (3) Passion. The power of the triangle! ✨"
- "#Leadership #Innovation #Success #Unstoppable"
- "I couldn't have done this alone. Grateful for my team! ❤️"
- "What I learned from this journey..."
- "I'm genuinely excited to announce..."
- Long inspirational takeaway after a brief win
- Every post ends with a lesson/wisdom

**Good social:**
- Tells a specific story
- Shows the actual win or struggle
- Admits uncertainty
- Includes a detail that's weird/true
- People recognize the person in it
- One emoji max, if any
- No hashtags or minimal hashtags
- Specific, not generic

## The Social Media Trap

LinkedIn culture encourages performance. AI amplifies this. The result: everyone sounds the same.

Real wins sound different because they come with:
- Specific numbers
- Actual struggles
- Honest uncertainty
- Details that don't fit the narrative

## Common Social AI Mistakes

**Mistake 1: The emoji overload**
> Just shipped v2! 🚀 So excited ✨ Can't believe it's live 💡 Grateful for the team ❤️ Let's go! 🔥

**Fix:**
> Just shipped v2. Load time dropped from 3s to 400ms. Team crushed it.

---

**Mistake 2: The three virtues**
> Three things made this possible: (1) Determination, (2) Innovation, (3) Teamwork. When you combine these, magic happens! ✨

**Fix:**
> We shipped it early because Sarah stayed late debugging, we used caching instead of rebuilding, and honestly I just kept bothering people until it worked.

---

**Mistake 3: The motivational wrap-up**
> [Brief win description] This taught me the importance of hard work, persistence, and believing in yourself. Remember: the only limits are the ones you set for yourself! 🌟

**Fix:**
> [Brief win description] We're doing it again next quarter. Might fail. We'll see.

---

**Mistake 4: Manufactured vulnerability**
> I'll be honest... *[pause]* ...we almost didn't make it. But we pushed through!

(This is fake vulnerability. Real vulnerability: the actual moment of doubt.)

**Fix:**
> We shipped late. For a week I thought we'd have to cancel. Then the team figured out the caching issue and we launched anyway.

---

**Mistake 5: Generic gratitude**
> Grateful for my amazing team for their hard work and dedication. You all inspire me every day! ❤️

**Fix:**
> Sarah debugged until 11pm. James redesigned the UI twice. Karim talked me off the ledge when I wanted to rewrite everything. That's why we shipped.

---

**Mistake 6: The hashtag wall**
> #Leadership #Innovation #Teamwork #Success #Growth #Mindset #Grateful #Blessed #Hustle #Unstoppable

**Fix:**
> (Maybe #startup if relevant. That's it.)

---

## Checkpoints Before Posting

- [ ] Is there a specific story (not generic)?
- [ ] Did I include at least one real number?
- [ ] Would someone who knows me recognize this as my voice?
- [ ] Did I avoid all emoji abuse (0-1 emoji max)?
- [ ] Did I avoid: passionate, grateful, thrilled, excited, learned, journey, inspired, transformation?
- [ ] Did I avoid motivational clichés?
- [ ] Is there a detail that's a bit weird or specific?
- [ ] Am I bragging a little? (That's OK)
- [ ] Am I admitting something that didn't work? (Credible)
- [ ] Does this need all these hashtags? (Probably not)

## Examples

### Career Win

**Before (AI):**
> 🚀 Thrilled to announce that I've just been promoted to VP of Engineering! After months of dedicated hard work and unwavering commitment, this moment represents a pivotal turning point in my career journey.
>
> Three people I'm grateful for: (1) My mentor Sarah who believed in me, (2) My team who inspired me daily, (3) My family who supported me.
>
> This journey taught me that with persistence, determination, and a commitment to excellence, anything is possible. I'm genuinely excited about what comes next!
>
> #Leadership #Growth #Grateful #Blessed

**After (Human):**
> I'm the new VP of Engineering.
>
> Honestly shocked. I was supposed to stay IC. Three things changed: Sarah pushed me to take it, the org was a mess and needed someone who could sit in the chaos, and apparently I don't say no enough.
>
> Excited and terrified. First week: figuring out why we have 15 Slack channels that do the same thing.

---

### Project Shipped

**Before (AI):**
> Just launched the new dashboard! 🎉 This has been a transformative project that represents months of innovative thinking and collaborative effort. I'm thrilled to share that our cutting-edge platform now seamlessly integrates advanced analytics capabilities.
>
> Three reasons this matters:
> 1. Innovation in the space
> 2. Value for customers  
> 3. Pride in the team
>
> Grateful for the incredible team who made this possible. Let's go! 🚀 ✨
>
> #Innovation #Teamwork #Success

**After (Human):**
> Shipped the dashboard. Query time went from 8s to 400ms.
>
> Not pretty but it works. Spent two weeks on performance tuning because the first version was dog slow. Will rebuild the UI next quarter when we know what users actually do with it.
>
> Team did good work. Karim found the bottleneck nobody saw.

---

### Struggle/Reflection

**Before (AI):**
> Reflecting on a challenging period in my career. While the journey was difficult, it taught me invaluable lessons about resilience, perseverance, and the transformative power of growth mindset.
>
> Key learnings: (1) Embrace challenges, (2) Believe in yourself, (3) Never give up.
>
> I'm grateful for this experience and excited for what the future holds. Remember: the only limits are the ones you set for yourself! 🌟
>
> #PersonalGrowth #Resilience #Mindset

**After (Human):**
> Got fired from my last role. Still figuring out what went wrong, but here's what I think: I was doing the job badly, manager gave me feedback I didn't listen to, and yeah, deserved it.
>
> Now at [new company] and it's different. Actually read feedback this time. Also: therapy helps.

---

### Learning Moment

**Before (AI):**
> Today I discovered an important truth about collaboration. After an enlightening conversation with a colleague, I realized that the power of teamwork and open communication can unlock unprecedented potential.
>
> Key insight: Listen more, talk less. Be vulnerable. Believe in your team.
>
> This moment transformed how I approach leadership. #Wisdom #Leadership #Growth

**After (Human):**
> I spent a year telling my team how to build stuff. Turns out they knew better than me and just weren't saying anything because I kept talking.
>
> Learned it when I shut up for a meeting and they ran the whole thing better than I would have.
>
> Now I talk less.

## Voice Calibration for Social Writers

Look for:
- **Specificity level** (vague? Exact numbers? Mix?)
- **Vulnerability pattern** (never admit struggles? Sometimes? Often?)
- **Humor type** (present? Absent? Dark? Self-deprecating?)
- **Story structure** (setup-problem-solution? Meandering? Specific moment?)
- **Emoji usage** (none? Thoughtful? Excessive?)
- **Hashtag usage** (none? Minimal? Walls?)
- **Authenticity tells** (weird details? Contradictions? Unresolved?)

Match in rewrites.

---

## LinkedIn Specific Rules

LinkedIn culture encourages performance. Counter it with:
- **Specificity** (3 million dollar increase, not "significant growth")
- **Honesty** (admit failures, not just wins)
- **Personality** (write like you talk)
- **Weird details** (the laptop you were using, the dumb joke someone made)
- **No hashtag walls** (1-2 max)
- **No emoji overload** (0-1 max)
- **No motivational endings** ("and then I learned to believe in myself" — ugh)

---

## Twitter/X Specific Rules

- Short is better
- One joke per tweet
- Specific > vague
- Link if you have something worth linking to
- Threads are OK but don't thread everything
- Retweet less, tweet more

---

## The Social Virtue

Real social media is you. Not a personal brand. Not a business vehicle. You.

The best posts show:
- A real win (with numbers)
- An actual struggle
- Your real humor
- Something weird
- A detail nobody expects

AI makes everyone sound the same. Your job is to sound like you.

Specificity is personality. Vagueness is performance.
