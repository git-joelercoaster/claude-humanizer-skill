## TEST 1: TECHNICAL DOCUMENTATION

### Sample (AI-generated)
> API Rate Limiting Guide
>
> Understanding rate limiting is absolutely critical for building scalable applications that leverage our platform's capabilities. This comprehensive guide unlocks the potential of our cutting-edge rate limiting system, which represents a paradigm shift in how developers approach API consumption.
>
> Rate Limiting Fundamentals
>
> Rate limiting serves as a testament to our commitment to platform stability and reliability. By implementing intelligent throttling mechanisms, we seamlessly integrate protection against abuse while maintaining optimal performance. This innovative approach has been proven to enhance resilience across enterprise deployments.
>
> The Three Tiers of Rate Limiting
>
> Our system implements three essential rate limit categories. First, we have request-based limits, which restrict the number of API calls per time window. Second, we have bandwidth-based limits, which constrain data transfer. Third, we have concurrent connection limits, which manage simultaneous requests. Each tier contributes to a comprehensive strategy for ensuring platform stability.
>
> Implementation Best Practices
>
> When implementing rate limiting, it's worth noting that developers should utilize exponential backoff strategies to gracefully handle throttled requests. Furthermore, one could argue that monitoring rate limit headers is crucial for understanding quota consumption patterns. Additionally, it's important to mention that proactive rate limit management can prevent cascading failures in your application.
>
> Conclusion
>
> In conclusion, rate limiting stands as a critical component of professional API integration. By understanding these best practices, you'll unlock the ability to build resilient applications that seamlessly scale with your user base.

### Identified Tells

**Significance inflation:**
- "absolutely critical" (artificial urgency)
- "cutting-edge" (unwarranted)
- "paradigm shift" (inflated)
- "represents a testament to" (puffing)
- "innovative approach"
- "stands as a critical component" (repeats significance at end)

**Cargo-cult buzzwords:**
- "leverage our platform's capabilities"
- "unlocks the potential"
- "seamlessly integrate"
- "intelligent throttling mechanisms"
- "unlock the ability to build resilient applications"

**Hedging overload:**
- "it's worth noting that"
- "one could argue that"
- "it's important to mention that"

**Rule-of-three:**
- "three essential rate limit categories" with exact three types listed

**Passive voice:**
- "are being throttled"
- "is constrained"

**Fake urgency:**
- "absolutely critical"
- "crucial for understanding"
- "prevent cascading failures"

**Generic conclusions:**
- "In conclusion" → repeats opening idea → closing with vague promise

## Summary of Test Cases

| Genre | AI Tells Density | Primary Issues |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Technical | HIGH | Significance puffing, buzzwords, hedging, rule-of-three |
| Interview | HIGH | Inflated claims, unsubstantiated expertise, fake humility |
| Personal Essay | VERY HIGH | Overstated life changes, fake drama, unearned sentimentality |
| Email | VERY HIGH | Buzzword overload, artificial urgency, vague requests |
| LinkedIn | VERY HIGH | Emoji abuse, motivational boilerplate, fake humility |
| Grant | VERY HIGH | Melodrama masquerading as urgency, inflated significance |
| Marketing | EXTREME | Fake urgency, 25+ buzzwords per section, FOMO tactics |

**Observation:** Marketing and LinkedIn are the densest AI, followed by grants and personal essays. Technical and interviews are high but more recoverable. All show clear patterns.
